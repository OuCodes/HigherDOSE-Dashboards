# Q1 Campaign Analysis: 2024 vs 2025
**Google & Meta Campaign Performance Comparison**

---

## Executive Summary

January 2025 shows **+34.0% YoY growth** in paid media spend compared to January 2024, with Meta leading the increase at +42% while Google grew +24%. Total Q1 2024 spend was $1.62M across Google and Meta platforms.

### Key Highlights
- **January 2025 Total Spend:** $835,703 (+34.0% vs Jan 2024)
- **Google Spend (Jan 2025):** $344,468 (+24.2% YoY)
- **Meta Spend (Jan 2025):** $491,234 (+41.9% YoY)
- **Platform Mix:** Google 41.2%, Meta 58.8% (slight shift toward Google vs 2024)

## Strategic Insights & Recommendations

### Platform Mix Evolution

**2024 Q1 Average:**
- Google: 39.2%
- Meta: 60.8%

**2025 January:**
- Google: 41.2% (+2.0pp)
- Meta: 58.8% (-2.0pp)

**Insight:** Slight rebalancing toward Google, potentially indicating better ROAS or strategic diversification.

### Campaign Strategy Observations

#### Google Strategy
1. **Brand Protection:** Heavy investment in branded search ensures competitive defense
2. **Performance Max Adoption:** Embracing automated bidding strategies
3. **Product-Level Granularity:** Dedicated campaigns for hero products
4. **Geographic Expansion:** Active investment in CA and UK markets

#### Meta Strategy
1. **ASC Heavy:** Leveraging Meta's AI-driven shopping campaigns extensively
2. **Dynamic Creative:** DPA/ASC campaigns allow for personalized product feeds
3. **Retention Focus:** Dedicated retention campaigns show CRM integration
4. **Rapid Testing:** Multiple campaign launches in January indicate aggressive testing

### Growth Opportunities

1. **Scale February/March:** January's strong performance suggests room for Q1 acceleration
2. **International Markets:** CA/UK showing promise; potential for expansion
3. **Product Diversification:** Sauna Blanket and Red Light campaigns performing well
4. **ASC Expansion:** Meta ASC campaigns driving volume; opportunities to expand product sets

### Risk Mitigation

1. **Platform Concentration:** 60% Meta allocation creates platform risk
2. **Brand Dependency:** Heavy Google brand spend may indicate competitive pressure
3. **Scaling Efficiency:** +34% spend increase requires MER monitoring
